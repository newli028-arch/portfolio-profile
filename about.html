My Passions & Interests

Gym

I found the gym through a powerlifter I once dated about two and half years ago. He had his own little gym in his garage where he tried showing me and teaching me about lifting. At first I hated it. I found it too sweaty, unamusing, and quite dirty hence from being in the garage. Eventually a few months later my parents got a Lifetime gym memebership where me and my family would frequently go to the gym to become healthier. With being in a real gym enviornment, I was able to enjoy myself. From there I picked up quickly. I taught myself most of the things that I know today by trial and error, the internet, and learning what was best for my body.

Having a powerlifter by my side etched my desire to become strong. I loved the gym because I wanted to push my limits and become my strongest self. I naturally began to fall in love with the entire process. With becoming my strongest self I learned there are ups and downs. Not every day in the gym will be a good day. You will have bad days but the most important thing is that you arrived and you tried. I have faced moments where I have plateued in my strength. These are moments where I don't feel strong but I have learned this is totally normal especailly if I am doing all the right things. I need to forgive my body and let it rest so recovery will be fast and I can achieve my goals. In my first semester of college, I suffered through what it seemed like a back injury that prevented me from moving let alone going to the gym. This made so sad because it had felt like someone had just taken the thing I loved from me away. There would be times I tried going and the pain just worsened which made me upset. It made me think of the times were I took my health for granted. This went on for months until I finally recovered enough where I felt healthy enough to go to the gym. Ever since then, I felt so blessed to have a healthy working body that I never wanted to take my body for granted and that I would always push myself to become my best self.

The gym has taught me discipline and structure. I consistently go even if I am not feeling the best or if I have feel defeated. I have learned to structure most of my day to day life through the gym. I eat enough fats, carbs, protein to help me feel energized and full throughout the day. I walk and move my body so that my food can digest properly. I sleep just enough for optimal recovery. I am consistent because the gym is a constant. I recieve exaclty what I put in. I want to continue this journy by maybe helping other with personal training but for now I would like to focus on myself and growing within.

Beauty

I first discovered cosmetics when I was thirteen years old. I had always been ugly kid who people always teased me about. I started out with a little mascara and eyeshadow. Even though it wasn't a lot, it still made me feel pretty. I self taught myself. I experiented with old makeup my sister gave me which I was very thankful for.

I had never really thought about going to school for cosmetology. That was until I took a course in high school. I got to experience the real work and it taught me so many skills. I loved it so much. My teachers encouraged me. They all though I was a natural especially when it came to the art. I was wonderful at paniting nails and doing makeup. It inspired me to become a cosmetologist.

Taking the course in cosmetology has inpired me to build a business with my best friend who is also just as passionate as I am. We are both currently on our career paths that are soon going to come together to create a salon business that helps those become their best selves and feel comfortable while doing so.
